The short version

Here is the encouraging read: the get.fluff.co funnel was never actually given a proper creative test, which means its potential is still on the table. The budget was there and the buyers were valuable. What was missing was a steady supply of fresh creative competing for that budget. Fix that one thing and we get a genuine answer on what this funnel can do.
  • Budget was never the problem. The funnel ran steadily at about $3,160 a day for 20 days. There was plenty of fuel.
  • After the one June batch, 93% of spend went to a single ad. That is scaling one creative, which is a very different thing from testing many.
  • No fresh creative was added after June 16. With nothing new entering the funnel, there was nothing for a test to discover.
  • It was switched off right as that one ad cooled. The plug was pulled before a real test ever got to run.
  • The opportunity is clear and simple. Feed the funnel a steady stream of fresh creative in a proper test structure, judged on real sales. That is a quick fix, and it is the whole game.

Proof 3 of 4 · the centerpiece

3. By the end, it was a one ad funnel

At a glance
This is the heart of it. By the final week a single ad was eating about 93% of the daily budget. That is scaling one bet, not running a test.
A real creative test spreads budget across several fresh ads and lets the winners earn more. Here the opposite happened: once the June batch launched, the budget collapsed onto a single static (ad 032). From June 17 to 24 that one ad took 89% to 97% of the entire funnel’s daily spend.

4. It was switched off at the low point

At a glance
The one ad carrying the funnel was tiring, dropping from 2.53 to 0.93 Meta ROAS. That is the moment it got switched off, not refreshed.
As the week went on the single ad cooled, from 2.53 Meta ROAS on June 20 down to 0.93 by June 24, roughly 0.49 in real terms. Instead of refreshing the creative at that moment, the funnel was wound down: the ad stopped after June 24, a little spend trailed into June 25, and it went quiet by June 26.

The opportunity

5. What a fair test would unlock

At a glance
Everything that was missing is in our control. Steady fresh creative in a real test structure, judged on real sales, is the whole fix.
We already have the budget, the buyers, and a creative engine. We just need to run them in a structure built to find winners.
  • A steady creative cadence. Fresh batches feeding in every week, not one batch in mid June and then silence.
  • A real test structure. Several creatives sharing budget on even footing so the best one can pull ahead on merit, rather than one ad quietly eating 93% of the spend.
  • Judged on real sales, not Meta. Read performance by reconciling the Stripe payment webhook against Shopify orders, since Meta’s own numbers run about double reality here.
  • Scale the proven winners. Once a creative earns it on real numbers, then and only then does it deserve the kind of budget 032 was handed on day one.
Concretely, the next step: a two-week creative test in ABO, a fresh Blitzads batch each week, four to six creatives per ad set on equal fixed budgets, each given enough spend to read a clear signal, judged on Stripe and Shopify. Then scale only what earns it.
The encouraging part: we have already seen this work. The sister landing page test, run with a proper structure, surfaced clear winners. Bring that same discipline to the get.fluff.co funnel and we finally learn what it is really worth.
Notes

How to read these numbers

At a glance
Two quick rules for everything above: halve Meta’s ROAS to get reality, and trust the live pull over the spreadsheet export.
  • Meta ROAS runs about double reality. A pixel shared across both ad accounts over-reported sales, so every Meta ROAS here should be roughly halved. Real performance is read from the Stripe payment webhook reconciled against Shopify orders; Meta is used here only for the shape of the trend.
  • This is the get.fluff.co CBO funnel only. The figures come from a live pull of that one campaign, so the daily spend, ROAS, and single ad concentration are exact.
  • One note on the exported file. The spreadsheet export filters to a handful of landing page URLs and leaves out ad 032 entirely, which is why an export-only view makes the budget look far smaller than it was. The live pull captures it in full.
  • What this report does not claim. It does not say the funnel is proven to work. It says the funnel was never given a fair test, so its real potential is still unknown. That is the gap worth closing.
