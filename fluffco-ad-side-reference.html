02What Moves CPA and ROAS

Three levers do the work

Cost per purchase is really impression cost divided by how well those impressions convert. The source account did not win by paying more for attention. It won by buying cheap impressions and being disciplined about what it scaled. Three levers, each of which the model in the next section is designed to pull.

01

Cheap impressions through statics, held down by a cost cap

The winning ads are static images running at $26 to $50 CPM, not high-cost video. The cost cap is what keeps that CPM low as spend grows, instead of letting delivery costs drift up. Cheap impressions that still convert are the whole game.

In the model, this is why the Scale campaign runs on Cost Cap
02

Concentrate budget on ads that already proved they convert

Money sits behind a short list of winners rather than being spread thin across untested ideas. New creative still gets its shot, but in a separate lane, so concentration never means standing still.

In the model, this is the split between Scale and Testing
03

Judge on link clicks and return, not headline click rate

All-clicks click rate flatters the wrong ads. One static showed a 25.95% all-clicks rate but only a 2.43% link click rate and a 1.18 return. The winners here have modest click rates and strong returns, which is what holds up when you spend behind them.

In the winners list, every ad is ranked on return and real purchase volume
03The Model

Three campaigns, one job each

The structure exists to pull all three levers at once. Every new creative enters Testing. Anything that shows a clear winner sign graduates into Scale, which runs on the cost cap and holds proven ads only. Landing pages are tested in parallel, with a steady control creative so the page is the only thing changing.

The promote trigger that makes this work

An asset graduates from Testing to Scale once it clears roughly 15 or more purchases at a return at or above the scaler target. That is why ads with real volume move up while a high return on three purchases waits. Having a number is what keeps Scale full of genuine winners and stops it from quietly turning into a third test campaign. Set the exact threshold to taste.

05How to Read These Numbers

So the reference is used safely

This ties back to lever three. A few source ads show very high returns, 10x and up, on a single purchase and a handful of dollars. That is noise, not a winner sign, and it does not trigger promotion. Every ad in the lists above either has real purchase volume or is clearly marked as still building. When an asset graduates to the cost cap, watch its first few days, since testing usually runs without a cap and performance can shift once one applies. And give any newly consolidated campaign time to finish learning before judging it.
