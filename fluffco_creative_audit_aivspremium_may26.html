The bottom line

No, Premium does not earn its 2x.

On the surface BlitzAds looks better (1.99 vs 1.90 ROAS). But that gap is one single legacy ad. Strip each side's biggest spender and the two are identical.

The $1 vs $2 production gap is real, but it is immaterial next to media spend (about $290 of difference against $100k spent). So the decision is not "is Premium better quality." It is "are we paying more for the same outcome." Right now, yes.

The "worth the $" trap. The $1 vs $2 tag invites a production-cost ROI sum: AI cost ~$158 to make 158 ads, Premium ~$448 for 224. But against $100k of media, a $290 production gap rounds to zero. The cost that actually decides this is the media you pour behind each creative, and there both sources return the same 1.88. Premium has to win on performance to be worth it, and on this window it does not.

Caveat: ROAS is Meta pixel value, not Shopify-reconciled. If Premium and AI pull different customer quality (refund rate, repeat purchase), the verdict could shift. That is the one check worth running before reallocating budget.

Q4 + Q5 · Scaling funnel

Broad test, narrow scale

Counting ads by spend tier exposes the clearest behavioral split between the two sources. Premium gets more creatives into testing, but fewer survive to heavy scale.

BlitzAds · AI

Reached $100+17 ads
Scaled to $500+11 ads

65% of its $100 ads push through to $500. AI creatives that work, work fast and get fed.

NER · Premium

Reached $100+26 ads
Scaled to $500+7 ads

Only 27% graduate. More shots on goal, lower conversion to scale.

Read it as a hit-rate problem, not a quality problem. Premium produces a wider top-of-funnel (26 vs 17 testable creatives) but its winners are harder to find. AI throws fewer darts and lands a higher share. If Premium creative is genuinely stronger, the leak is in selection or early kill decisions, not the asset itself. Worth auditing how fast Premium ads are being cut before they find their audience.
Method & caveats
  • Categorization: "Blitzads" in ad name → AI (BlitzAds). "NER_" or "FLU_" → Premium (NER). BlitzAds takes priority where a name contains both. Everything else → Other, excluded ($2,226, 9 ads).
  • Spend window is the 30 days May 3 to Jun 1 2026. Dates inside ad names are original creation dates, so several top spenders are legacy creatives still running profitably.
  • ROAS, CPA and AOV use Meta "Purchases conversion value" (pixel-attributed), not Shopify-reconciled revenue. Treat as directional.
  • "Worth the $" is judged on performance, not production cost, because the $1 vs $2 production delta (~$290) is immaterial against ~$100k in media spend.
  • ROAS bars and the ex-outlier comparison use full account aggregation per source, not per-day averaging.
