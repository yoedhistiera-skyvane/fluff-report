How many assets, and how far they got

This is the part that was hard to see before. Each source ran a different number of creatives, and the two filter down very differently from "made" to "scaled." Spend is split nearly evenly ($51.0k vs $49.7k), so volume is the real difference, not budget.

Bars show each source's count as a share of its own creatives run, so the steep drop is visible. Most assets never get going: 131 of 158 BlitzAds and 145 of 224 NER creatives never cleared even $10 before being cut. That is normal in paid social, the job is finding the few that break out.

The signal here: the two get to a similar overall efficiency by different routes. Premium tests across more creatives (26 reaching $100 vs 17) while AI concentrates spend into fewer, harder-scaling ones. Of the creatives that earned a real test, about 2 in 3 of BlitzAds went on to scale versus about 1 in 4 for NER, so Premium's pattern is broader testing with a more selective scale step. Worth knowing if the team wants to tune how quickly creatives are judged.

Best performers

The account leans on a tiny handful

By raw spend, BlitzAds owns the top (11 of the top 20 spenders). By ROAS above $300 spend, it is a dead heat (10/10), and NER holds the single most efficient ad. The bigger story is concentration: the top 3 creatives carry 78% of BlitzAds spend and 73% of NER spend. The account is not broadly healthy, it is propped up by 4 to 6 ads on both sides.

Method & caveats
  • Categorization: "Blitzads" in the ad name maps to AI (BlitzAds). "NER_" or "FLU_" maps to Premium (NER). BlitzAds takes priority where a name contains both. Everything else is Other and excluded ($2,226 across 9 ads).
  • "382 creatives" is 158 BlitzAds plus 224 NER unique ad names, with no overlap between sources. A creative that ran in several ad sets (14 of them) is counted once, with its spend summed, so it is not inflated.
  • Duplication: 7 ads carry a "Copy" suffix and 4 of those have the original running alongside, so distinct assets are about 156 BlitzAds and 221 NER. The copies kept their own spend (it was really spent), so no performance metric changes.
  • Spend window is the 30 days from May 3 to Jun 1 2026. Dates inside ad names are creation dates, so several top spenders are older creatives still running.
  • ROAS, cost per purchase and AOV use Meta "Purchases conversion value" (pixel-attributed), not Shopify-reconciled revenue. Treat as directional.
  • "Worth the $" is judged on performance, not production cost, because the $1 vs $2 production delta (about $290) is immaterial against roughly $100k of media spend.
